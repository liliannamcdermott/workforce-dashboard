Section 2 · People Program Design

The Catalyst
HiPo Program

A targeted retention program for the organization's highest-potential early-tenure employees, designed to be lean, measurable, and held to a strict 12-month ROI test.

✅ Recommendation

Launch — with discipline. Narrow targeting (top ~15–20% of Year 1–2 employees), low-cost high-signal interventions, and a clear 12-month ROI gate. Do not build broadly. Build a lean, measurable pilot.

Key Assumptions & Sensitivity
What drives the recommendation — and what could change it

The recommendation rests on four assumptions: that about 20% of Year 1–2 leavers are genuinely high performers; that the program achieves a 20% reduction in that group's attrition (the base case between 5% pessimistic and 35% optimistic); that replacing one employee costs ~$75,000 (1× blended salary); and that the program runs for ~$200K/year. The most important sensitivity is the exit driver question — if employees are leaving primarily because of pay, this program will not work, and that $200,000 is better redirected to compensation adjustments.

🚨 Before Launching — Collect This First

Exit interview data is the single most important input we don't yet have. If compensation drives >50% of early exits, the budget is better spent on targeted pay adjustments. Everything else in the model is directionally stable. This one assumption can flip the recommendation.

Program Design: The Catalyst Program
How the program works, who qualifies, and how we measure it.

Eligibility — Both Gates Required

Gate 1 — Tenure Window

6–18 months of tenure at time of nomination. Targets the highest-risk departure window while giving managers enough time to assess performance.

Gate 2 — Manager Nomination Scorecard

(1) Output consistently exceeds expectations · (2) Proactively takes on scope beyond role · (3) Demonstrates leadership with peers · (4) Assessed as a flight risk within 12 months

Cohort: top ~15–20% of eligible employees. Target 30–50 participants in Year 1. HiPo status communicated as a development opportunity — not a public badge.

Measuring Success

12-Month Retention Rate
HiPo cohort vs. matched control group. Target: ≥15% above control. Reviewed at Month 12.
Participant Engagement Score
eNPS vs. company baseline. Target: ≥15 points above average. Collected quarterly.
Program Satisfaction
Participant and sponsor rating. Target: ≥4.0 out of 5.0. Collected at each cycle end.
Continuation Gate

At Month 10, People & HR presents ROI to CHRO + CFO. Program continues only if cohort shows ≥15% higher retention than control. No automatic renewal.

Program Components
01
Senior Sponsor
Each participant is paired with a VP+ sponsor outside their direct chain for one 45-minute 1:1 per quarter, covering strategic context, visibility, and candid feedback.
02
Individual Development Plan
Co-created within 30 days of program entry. Includes one primary growth goal, one stretch project, and one skill gap to close. Reviewed each quarter.
03
Cohort Learning & Networking
Four 90-minute sessions per year: two internal (leader panels, strategy) and two external (facilitated skill-building). Builds cross-functional peer networks.
04
Quarterly Pulse Check
A 15-minute structured check-in between the participant and their People Partner, covering IDP progress, sponsor quality, and engagement signals. Flags at-risk participants early.

AI Tool · Manager Check-In Assistant

1:1 Agenda
Builder

An AI agent that helps employees build structured, professional agendas for their manager 1:1s — surfacing attrition risk signals before they reach resignation.

Problem

Most attrition signals go undetected until the decision to leave is already made. Managers lack a structured way to surface them in normal 1:1s.

Solution

A guided AI agent that walks employees through building an agenda — collecting updates, bandwidth signals, and housekeeping items — then sends it to their manager.

Why It Works Here

This tool works best at mission-driven organizations where employees are genuinely invested in their work. The 1:1 format is most effective when both parties want the conversation to go well — and that condition tends to already exist in environments like this.
